I am a Doctoral Assistant at **EPFL** in Lausanne, currently in the final year of my Ph.D. within the [Laboratory of Computational Solid Mechanics](https://www.epfl.ch/labs/lsms/). 
My research, supported by the [MSCA GlobaLeaders Fellowship](https://www.epfl.ch/education/phd/doctoral-studies-structure/customized-curricula/epflglobaleaders/), focuses on the intersections of granular physics, tribology, and soft matter.

### Research Philosophy

My primary goal is to improve the physical and engineering understanding of complex mechanical/material systems.
To achieve this, I employ different computational and mathematical approaches:

- **High-Performance Computing** (HPC): I use large-scale simulations (LAMMPS/Tamaas/Jax) to explore fundamental physics in granular flows and contact mechanics  
- **Surrogate & ML Modeling**: I integrate data-driven methodologies, such as Gaussian Process Regression (GPR), differentiable physics and neural networks, to build efficient models
  
### Professional Experience

My perspective has been shaped by diverse international and industrial collaborations:

- Industrial R&D: I take part in a project with [**Bühler AG**](https://www.buhlergroup.com/global/de/homepage.html) to optimize industrial grain processing and conducted a research internship at [**ASML**](https://www.asml.com/en) within the Tribology and Surface Science group.
- Institutional Research: I have held research positions at the National Institute of Nuclear Physics ([**INFN**](https://www.infn.it/en/)) in Italy and participated in the [**EPFL Excellence in Engineering (E3)**](https://eee.epfl.ch/) program

### Community Engagement
